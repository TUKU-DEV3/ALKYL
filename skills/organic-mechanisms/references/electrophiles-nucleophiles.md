## Nucleophilicity

### Polar Protic Solvents (H‚āāO, ROH, RCOOH)
Solvation stabilizes small/hard anions more ‚Üí nucleophilicity follows **polarizability**:

```
I‚ĀĽ > Br‚ĀĽ > Cl‚ĀĽ > F‚ĀĽ
RS‚ĀĽ >> RO‚ĀĽ
```

### Polar Aprotic Solvents (DMSO, DMF, acetone, MeCN, THF)
No H-bonding solvation ‚Üí nucleophilicity follows **basicity**:

```
F‚ĀĽ > Cl‚ĀĽ > Br‚ĀĽ > I‚ĀĽ
RO‚ĀĽ > RS‚ĀĽ
```

**Key consequence**: F‚ĀĽ, CN‚ĀĽ, N‚āÉ‚ĀĽ, RO‚ĀĽ are far better nucleophiles in DMSO/DMF than in water. SN2 reactions are dramatically faster in polar aprotic solvents.

### Nucleophilicity Ranking (general, polar aprotic)
```
RS‚ĀĽ ‚Čą I‚ĀĽ > CN‚ĀĽ ‚Čą N‚āÉ‚ĀĽ > Br‚ĀĽ > RO‚ĀĽ ‚Čą HO‚ĀĽ > Cl‚ĀĽ > F‚ĀĽ > H‚āāO ‚Čą ROH
R‚āāNH > RNH‚āā > NH‚āÉ > ArNH‚āā
```

### Nucleophile vs. Base
A nucleophile attacks carbon (C‚ÄďX bond). A base attacks hydrogen (C‚ÄďH or X‚ÄďH bond).
Same species, different role ‚ÄĒ determined by substrate and steric context.
